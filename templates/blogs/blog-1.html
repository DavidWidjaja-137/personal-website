Making this personal website

June 2023

It is actually very easy to have a personal website these days. If you want nothing more than a blog, you can get yourself a managed WordPress site on Squarespace or Wix or something else. If you know a bit of frontend and Git, and you want something free, use GitHub Pages and an open-source template. For most use cases, a combination of LinkedIn, Instagram and (maybe) Substack, Tumblr, or Medium, provides enough of a public outlet that a personal website becomes redundant.

Why have a personal website?

I think that having a personal website fulfils a niche that no social media account can fill. When you post pictures on Instagram, share an update on LinkedIn, or publish a blog on Medium, your work goes through an algorithm that evaluates who will see it, and when it will appear on their feed. When you write for an audience who are passively being handed content, your writing will tend towards highlights, summaries, and digests. Only sensational content can make an impression within an endlessly scrolling sea of information.

A personal website is different. When someone enters on your personal website, they are looking for you and what you have to say. Yes, maybe most of those people will end up being background-checkers from HR and bots, but perhaps some of them will be interested in hearing what you have to say, not just what you are obliged to write. And so, I think building a personal website is a chance to start a real connection.

Why build a personal website from scratch?

Mostly for the experience of it. I've studied frontend, but until now I never really applied it to something I actually wanted to do. I know roughly how HTTPS works, but what good is that if I never tried to use it? I've gotten some AWS certifications and played around with Terraform, but I've only done stuff for work with it.

What is the current tech stack?

I am changing the tech stack constantly to try new things. But currently:

  • Terraform for Infrastructure-as-Code
  • Python Flask as the framework + Gunicorn as the webserver
  • Jinja2 as the templating system
  • Heroku as a Platform-as-a-service

Why no web framework >:|

This is a static website, didn't need one.
